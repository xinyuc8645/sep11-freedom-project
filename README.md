# SEP11 Freedom Project
by Xin Yu Chen & Rain Song(Partner Peer)

## Context
I am a student at [HSTAT](https://www.hstat.org/) in the [Software Engineering Program](https://hstatsep.github.io/). The "Freedom Project" for SEP11 is a year-long project all about making something using JavaScript alongside with an tool chosen from their own choose; for example in this case phaser.

For my project, I chose to collaborate and work in pairs with one of my peers using the tool of phaser and others sources including, outside research sources(youtube, phaser documentations) and tinkering with the tools ourselves in order to create an game that is based off of dungeon genre

### What is SEP11 and how was it different from SEP10/What did you learn in SEP11?
I'm going to be real, SEP11 is ALOT way more different than what SEP11 had to offer. Back in SEP10 we only learned about basic HTML including website structures, basic CSS, bootstrap and basically let's call that frontend of the the web-design and coding. Whereas compare it to SEP11 its more of like the backend involved. For example you get to learn aobut Javascript, how you can directly change something in an website, we also experienced with p5js which is more of like an creative aspect but still lot's of fun. However, by the end goal of SEP10 and SEP11, the objective still remains the same. Freedom project, you learn the tool you use and you create an game/website(whatever you can think of only limitation is SEP10 in which only website is allowed and 3d stuff) and then you showcase it off during the end of year expo.
### SEP11 Freedom Project
Earlier section, I mentioned that SEP11's freedom project and SEP10's freedom project was similar, nothing too much different. Since the over-arching goal is to create something you are passioning about and using the tool you tinkered with in order to creat that end result. Once again to elaborate, there is main stages. First you choose an tool, ranging from easy, medium and to hard. Then you tinkered with the tool that you choose for a couple of months. Then you create an MVP(minimum viable product) of the tool you tinkered with and something you are passioned about, then you will give an in-class presentation about your tool, your MVP, then lastly you show it off at the expo. And also SEP11 you have a bit more freedom than SEP10 in terms of ideas

## Implications For Impact Our Game Have Towards The Society
##### PROS
###### Global
* (Environmental wise)Our game could help reduce and lower the physical demanded needed for traditional board games, books and other entertainment that would require usage of physical materials, as our game are digital and does not require anything to be wasted, meaning there would be a lesser need for physical materials like papers,plastics, metals, woods and others to be used and then wasted if thrown out.
* (Governmennt/Law wise) The dungeon game can be show and and educate younger audience of how the real life work and functions. Since the basic premise of the dungeon game is to collect coins, collect key to unlock door, and to avoid spike so you don't died and restart. In which this reflects the real life, since think of it like this: spike=restart, meaning in real life law and rules=are made to keep people in check and regulate people, so it does not disturb the flow of the society. Additionally the spike also can teach them what will happen if they step on it, just like real life law. If they step on spike, then they go back to beginning, if they broke a law, they get fined or worse end up in jail
* (Big systems wise) I think our game, even though its an basic version of popular dungeon games serve as an basic template or an starting point in which many developers started their project off from. Since like many popular dungeon games, for example fireboy andd watergirl uses the concept of player having to using their key's to move their characters, collecting stuff, solving puzzles and etc. Even though these concept may seem simple, it still are used in many popular and advanced dungeon games. By which implicating these basic ideas, later they can elaborate and add on to these simple ideas to make it more exciting and more complex.
* (Environemental Wise) Due to our game being virtual and not physical, it help reduce the carbon emission needed for an regulate game or entertainment game to be delivered. Since virtual games often don't need to be packaged, nor delivered via transportation's like trucks and etc. Meaning instead of being delivered and transported that causes carbon emissions, people can actually reduce that chance and help make the environment more cleaner.
###### Social
* (Inviduality) Can help an invidiual improve and develop an more complex mindset, through the use of answering question provided and navigating and solving puzzles. So that in real life, if they encounter an difficult problem their, immmediate thought aren't "getting the answer straight-away" but more of HOW and WHAT can he do to solve that problem themselves.
* (Relationships) Even though the dungeon game is yet to be multiplayer or co-op, it can help fuel the excitement and encourage teamwork between one person and other. Since our game's last level is hard, designed not to be beaten under one try. Therefore one person may have one trick whereas the other may have another trick up their sleeve.
* (Culture) Strongly encourages creativity within games, since our dungeon game is creative but I wouldn't say creative enough. But our game can serve as an creavitity starting point for those who want to further more step up to become a developer for an dungeon game that creates many challenges
###### Economic
* (Jobs) Our game can help encourage/persuaive those who might want to puruse in the fields of technologies, as in the current age of modern technologies our society has evolved a lot in the last couple of years and techonlogies right now are blooming and looks as if will take over the world in the next couple of years.
* (Fianance) Even though our game does not yet earn money nor derserve as an "real game" it can still help demonstrate and serve as an template for those who want to become a game developer and may contribute in the future of how successful game developing and using complexity in their game leads to revenvues and sales
* (Infrastructure) Because our game heavily relies virutually, this means that our game and other games that are virutally relies heavily on the server platforms and other software website, in order to both maintain and create the game
##### CONS
###### Global
* (Government/Laws) Since our game is virtually online, it can develop excessive screen time addiction which if it too much, may forced the government to enforce and regulate an more striciter law that would reduce screen time
* (Big Systems) Many games like ours depend on the internet and is online, and this DOES NOT mean that our game does not have any bug or in anyway is perfect and that is the case for all game there is out there. Meaning dependence on online techonlogies causes and is vulernable to bugs and glitches that are way in-depth and cannot be seen on the surface
* (Environemnt) Increased electricity usage from the virtual online game leads to more carbon emissions
###### Social
* (Invidual) The dungeon game that we created virtually online, yes can be fun and cure boredom. BUT can lead to excessive amount of screen time than the normal time. * (Jobs) Even though our dungeon game may serve as an template for those developers who are wanting to create an more advance game, it can create an mis-understanding in between for the person. Because they could think that "this game look easy to make and I would earn lot's of money instantly" which is usually not the case and it depends on the role they work on
